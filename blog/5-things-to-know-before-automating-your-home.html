5 Things to Know Before Automating Your Home

March 15, 2026 | 8 min read

Smart home automation has transformed from a luxury feature into an accessible investment for homeowners across Sarasota, Manatee, and Charlotte Counties. Whether you're considering upgrading your current home or planning automation in a new build, understanding the fundamentals before you begin will save you money, time, and frustration. Here are five essential things every homeowner should know before automating their home.

1. Start with a Clear Purpose and Prioritize Your Needs

Before selecting equipment or platforms, define what you want to achieve. Are you primarily interested in convenience, energy savings, security, or all of the above? Many homeowners make the mistake of trying to automate everything at once, leading to overwhelmed systems and wasted budget.

The most successful smart home implementations begin with a priority list. For example, you might decide that security and climate control are your top priorities, with lighting as a secondary consideration. This clarity allows you to allocate your budget efficiently and choose systems that excel in your priority areas. A professional smart home consultation can help you identify which automation features will deliver the most value for your lifestyle and home.

2. Budget Realistically and Plan for Professional Installation

Smart home automation costs vary significantly based on the scope of your project. A simple setup with a few smart bulbs and a connected thermostat might run $500-$1,500, while a comprehensive whole-home automation system can range from $5,000 to $25,000 or more, depending on your home's size and complexity.

While DIY options exist, professional installation is worth the investment for most homeowners. Proper installation ensures your systems work seamlessly together, are safely integrated with your home's electrical infrastructure, and are configured to your specific needs. In our experience across the Gulf Coast, homes that received professional installation reported significantly higher satisfaction rates and fewer technical issues over time. Most professionals, including those at All Things Automated, charge $90 per hour for residential work, so factor this into your budget accordingly.

3. Choose the Right Platform for Your Needs and Future Growth

The platform you select—whether it's Lutron, Control4, Google Home, or another system—will influence your flexibility, features, and long-term costs. This is one of the most critical decisions you'll make, yet many homeowners overlook it in favor of the cheapest upfront option.

Different platforms excel in different areas. Lutron systems are renowned for lighting precision and reliability, while Control4 offers comprehensive whole-home integration. Your choice should reflect both your current needs and your vision for the future. If you think you might expand your system in three years, ensure your chosen platform supports that growth. Consider ease of use, integration with devices you already own, and whether the platform will be supported long-term. We recommend discussing platform options with a professional who can explain the tradeoffs in language that makes sense for your home and goals.

4. Understand the Professional vs. DIY Trade-Off

DIY smart home systems are appealing because they're affordable and give you flexibility. However, they come with tradeoffs that many homeowners don't anticipate:

  • Integration challenges: Getting different devices to work smoothly together often requires technical troubleshooting.
  • Limited features: Many DIY platforms don't support advanced automation or seamless control across different systems.
  • Maintenance burden: You're responsible for updates, troubleshooting, and support when issues arise.
  • Resale value: Professional installations often add measurable value to your home, while basic DIY setups may not.

Professional installations handle all of this complexity for you and typically include ongoing support. For most Sarasota homeowners who want reliability and comprehensive automation, professional installation delivers better value despite the higher upfront cost.

5. Plan for the Future and Consider Your Home's Infrastructure

Smart home systems installed today should be flexible enough to accommodate new technology over the next five to ten years. Technology evolves rapidly, and your system should be designed with this in mind.

Additionally, consider your home's infrastructure. Older homes in historic Sarasota may have older wiring that affects what's possible without significant upgrades. Newer homes often have better infrastructure for running automation systems. If you're building new or planning major renovations, this is the ideal time to plan your automation infrastructure—it's significantly easier and cheaper to integrate systems during construction.

Also think about user experience. Will everyone in your household—from tech-savvy teenagers to less technology-oriented family members—be able to use the system? The best smart home system is one that everyone actually uses.

Moving Forward with Confidence

Smart home automation offers tremendous benefits in convenience, security, comfort, and energy efficiency. By thinking through these five considerations before you begin, you'll make decisions that align with your needs, budget, and long-term vision for your home.
